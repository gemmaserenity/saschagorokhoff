Why This Book

Written by a Man Who Lived Every Word

Most leadership books are written by researchers, academics, or professional authors who studied their subject from a distance. This book is different. Every principle in From Victim to Victory was first lived by Sascha Gorokhoff — and only then codified into a form others can apply. The book is autobiography, philosophy, and operating system — all compressed into a single volume.

Sascha was born with a terminal medical diagnosis. He was told he would be paralyzed, mentally impaired, and dead before age 7. He endured maternal abuse from birth to age 34, and years of severe school bullying. By every statistical prediction, his life should have ended in tragedy. Instead, he skied the Swiss Alps, earned three academic degrees in 2.5 years with honors, became a political delegate at federal and cantonal level in Switzerland, earned his Executive Master at IHEID Geneva, and now coaches Fortune 500 CEOs in four languages.

That journey — how he did it, what he learned, what you can do too — is the book.

What's Inside

The Core Principles

From Victim to Victory is built around the core principles Sascha has tested in his own life against conditions that would have broken most human beings. These are not theoretical — they are operational. Each section of the book dismantles a limiting pattern and installs the expanded architecture that replaced it in Sascha's own life.

I

The Choice at the Center of Everything

Why the line between victim and victory is not circumstantial — it is decisional. How the moment of choice works, how to recognize it, and how to make it consistently. Drawn from Sascha's own impossible survival of a medical death sentence.

II

Defying the Memo You Received

Every human being receives a "memo" at birth — about what is and isn't possible for them. Most accept it without ever reading it carefully. This section teaches you how to identify and reject the memo you were given. With examples from Sascha's own refusal of every medical prediction made about him.

III

Building on Broken Foundation

What happens when your foundation — family, childhood, physical body — was compromised before you ever had a say. How Sascha rebuilt from a foundation of medical adversity, maternal abuse, and social isolation — and the specific architecture required to build something extraordinary on a base that was meant to collapse.

IV

The Architecture of Resilience

Resilience is not a trait — it is a structure. Drawing from his IMD High Performance Leadership training and 30+ years of direct experience, Sascha breaks down the internal architecture that allows any human being to operate at peak capacity under maximum pressure. Specific frameworks. Operational principles.

V

Victory Is a Practice

Victory is not an event. It is not something you achieve and then own. Victory is a daily practice — a way of choosing, seeing, and moving through the world that must be renewed every single day. This section is the operating discipline for sustaining victorious living across decades.

VI

The Sovereignty Beyond Success

There is a level beyond success that most high achievers never reach — and that most coaches cannot teach because they've never been there. Sovereignty: the state in which external outcomes no longer determine internal experience. The final destination of the Victim-to-Victory journey.
