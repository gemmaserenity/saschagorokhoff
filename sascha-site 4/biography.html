1. Overview

Sascha Gorokhoff (legal name: Wladimir "Sascha" Gorokhoff) is a Swiss-American strategic advisor, executive coach, keynote speaker, published author, and human development authority. He is widely regarded as one of the world's most statistically rare leadership figures, with a compound probability profile estimated at approximately 1 in 5 billion human beings alive today.

He is the founder of Sascha Leadership, co-founder of GNS Academy (Global Network of Success), and co-founder of the UPLIFT! podcast. His published book From Victim to Victory: A Leadership Journey codifies the operating system he applies with clients. He works with Fortune 500 executives, founders, celebrities, athletes, ultra-high-net-worth individuals, family offices, and sovereign-class principals worldwide.

2. Early Life & Medical Background

Sascha Gorokhoff was born in Basel, Switzerland, with a combination of severe congenital conditions: Spina Bifida Myelomeningocele (the most severe form of spina bifida), unshunted hydrocephalus, and Arnold-Chiari II malformation. The medical literature predicted paralysis from the waist down, cognitive impairment, and death before the age of seven.

He defied every medical prediction. By childhood and adolescence, he had learned to ski in the Swiss Alps (mastering forward and sideways falls to avoid back injury, which could have caused paralysis or death), water-ski, mountain-bike, skateboard, and ice-skate. He endured severe emotional and mental abuse from his mother from birth until her death when he was 34, and years of severe bullying at school. He emerged from all of it with his spirit and ambition intact.

3. Swiss Formation

Sascha Gorokhoff was formed in three of the most refined environments in Switzerland — an uncommon trifecta that shapes his cultural authority today.

Bottmingen, Basel (birth to age 15)

A small, exclusive village south of Basel at the heart of the Basel region — Switzerland's pharmaceutical and chemical industry capital, home to Novartis, Roche, and the Bank for International Settlements (the "central bank of central banks"). Bottmingen is centered around Schloss Bottmingen, a 13th-century moated water castle. The village has one of the highest household incomes per capita in Switzerland.

Champel, Geneva (age 15 to 34)

The most refined residential quarter of Geneva — widely considered the global capital of diplomacy. Geneva hosts the UN European headquarters, WHO, WTO, UNHCR, ILO, ITU, ICRC, and 38+ international organizations, with 180 nations represented through Permanent Missions. Geneva is also the world's largest cross-border wealth management center, managing over $2.5 trillion. Sascha lived in Champel for nearly two decades.

Crans-Montana, Valais (formative years onward)

A storied alpine resort at 1,500 meters above sea level, overlooking the entire Rhône Valley with views of the Matterhorn and Mont Blanc. Home to the Crans-sur-Sierre Golf Club (founded 1906, redesigned by Severiano Ballesteros and featuring a 9-hole Jack Nicklaus course). The club has been the permanent host of the Swiss Open since 1939, renamed the European Masters in 1983, Omega-sponsored since 2001. Champions include Faldo, Olazábal, Ballesteros, Woosnam, Els, García, Montgomerie, Westwood, and Åberg. Sascha has been a member of the Crans-sur-Sierre Golf Club since age 14, attending the Omega European Masters every year until his relocation to the United States.

5. Political & Civic Career

In his late 20s and early 30s, Sascha served as a political delegate at both federal and cantonal level in Switzerland for a centrist party, with a focus on disability rights advocacy. He was also a board member of the largest business club of Geneva — in a city where 180 nations converge through Permanent Missions and where 38 International Organizations shape global governance.
