Born to Defy Every Prediction

Sascha Was Born Severely Disabled

Sascha was born in Basel with the worst form of Spina Bifida — Myelomeningocele — along with unshunted hydrocephalus and Arnold-Chiari II malformation. The medical literature gave this combination a grim prognosis: paralysis, cognitive impairment, and death before the age of 7. He defied every single prediction.

He also endured unimaginable mental and emotional abuse from his own mother from birth until her death when he was 34, and years of severe bullying at school. The weight of these cumulative experiences would have crushed the spirit of almost any human being. Sascha defied all odds. By sheer force of will, he emerged — forging a path of resilience, self-discovery, and triumph that now serves as a living masterclass for everyone he works with.

This is not inspirational content. This is the foundation of why everything Sascha teaches works — because it was earned at a cost most humans will never face. And it is the first layer of the 1 in 5 billion.
Physically Defying the Impossible

He Went Skiing.

Sascha learned to ski in the Swiss Alps. He learned to fall forward and sideways before learning to hold straight — knowing that falling on his back could mean paralysis or death. He skied anyway. He water-skied, mountain-biked, skateboarded, and ice-skated.

Why does this matter for the people who work with him? Because Sascha understands at a cellular level that the most dangerous limitations are not physical — they are the ones we accept in our mind before we ever try. His job is to make you unlearn the limits you never should have believed in the first place.
