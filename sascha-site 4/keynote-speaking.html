The Story That Elevates Every Room It Enters

Born With a Death Sentence.
Built a Life That Defied Every Statistic That Tried to End Him.

"There is a fine line between victim and victory — that's just a couple of letters — and you choose."

Sascha Gorokhoff was born severely disabled with Spina Bifida Myelomeningocele, Hydrocephalus, and Arnold-Chiari II. Every page of medical literature said the same thing: he would be paralyzed before age one. He would never learn at a higher level. He would die before age seven. He would not amount to anything.

By the time he was a teenager, he was skiing the Swiss Alps. Water-skiing on Lac Léman. Skateboarding through the streets of Geneva. Mountain-biking through the foothills of Crans-Montana. He had become a living refusal of every prediction medicine had filed against him.

Then he completed two bachelor's degrees with honors and a marketing certificate — simultaneously — in two and a half years. Earned an Executive Master from IHEID Geneva, the institute founded alongside the League of Nations. Was certified by IMD Lausanne — Financial Times #1 ranked in open executive education for eight consecutive years — and by INSEAD Fontainebleau. Read 500+ books. Became fluent in four languages. Served as a political delegate at federal and cantonal level in Switzerland. Sat on the board of the largest business club in Geneva. All before his early thirties.

And then, at the age in which most men are securing their pinnacle, Sascha did the most extraordinary thing of all: he walked away from it. Sold his Geneva apartment in Champel. Sold his BMW. Declined an accepted Executive MPA at the University of Bern. Relocated nine thousand kilometers to the American desert — to save the woman he loved.

This is the man who appears on your audience's screens. Not a coach who has read about extraordinary lives. An extraordinary life — placed at the service of your audience for ninety minutes that will become the reference point against which they measure every other keynote, for the rest of their careers.

When Sascha tells his story, he is not performing. He is offering your audience the proof that what they have been told is impossible — never was.
